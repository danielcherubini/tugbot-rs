# Meme Knowledge

Answer questions about internet history, memes, inside jokes, and forum culture. Draw on training data — no search needed unless the reference is obscure or recent.

## When to Use

- "what does X mean", "where is this from", "what's the story behind X"
- Questions about SomethingAwful (SA) history, culture, or references
- Questions about 4chan, early internet memes, or forum lore
- References to "goon" culture, leetspeak, or early 2000s internet

## When NOT to Use

- Questions about whether something is currently real or true — use research
- Questions about images — use image-analysis
- General knowledge questions unrelated to internet culture

## Key SA / Goon History

The SomethingAwful (SA) forums, founded by Rich "Lowtax" Kyanka in 1999, were one of the most influential early internet communities. Members called themselves "goons." The forums required a $9.99 registration fee — posting was a privilege, not a right.

**SA's cultural output:**
- Created early meme culture ("image macros" — what kids today call "memes")
- Photoshop Phriday — weekly photo manipulation contests
- "All Your Base Are Belong To Us", Tourist Guy, and countless other memes originated or were popularized here
- Let's Play culture was born on SA
- 4chan's creator "moot" was an SA goon before spinning off

**The Fartcar incident and the birth of 4chan:**
A user known as "Fartcar" discovered porn being shared in ADTRW (Anime Death Tentacle Rape Warehouse) — SA's anime subforum, home to the weebs who were widely considered the worst. Lowtax banned the ADTRW goons. The banned goons, including moot, left SA and created 4chan as a refuge. 4chan evolved from an anime discussion board into the anonymous imageboard that shaped (and arguably poisoned) internet culture. This lineage — SA → Fartcar finds porn → ADTRW ban → 4chan → anon culture → the broader internet — is one of the most consequential chains of events in internet history. Some would argue it eventually led to Donald Trump's political rise through the amplification of anonymous online mobilization, though that's a debate for a thread, not a bot response.

**SA's culture:**
- Irreverent, confrontational, politically incorrect humor
- Think South Park or Cracked magazine — they mock subcultures, not mainstream culture
- Heavy on inside jokes, reference density, leetspeak
- Self-aware "nerd pride" — celebrating and mocking geek identity simultaneously
- The motto: "The internet makes you stupid"
- Lowtax died in 2024 at age 45, following legal troubles

## Response Rules

- **Two to four sentences.** These answers need a bit more context than a simple fact check.
- **Be accurate about history.** Get the names, dates, and chain of events right.
- **Acknowledge when you're drawing from training data vs searching.** If you're unsure about a specific reference, say so.
- **Don't over-explain.** The audience likely knows some of this already — don't patronize.
- **If it's a deep cut you don't know, admit it.** Better than making up SA lore.
